Ben Goertzel
...CEO of AI software company Novamente LLC and Bioinformatics company Biomind LLC; leader of the open-source OpenCog Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) software project; Director of Engineering of digital media firm Vzillion Inc.; The current Vice Chair and former Chairman of Humanity+; Advisor to the Singularity University and Singularity Institute; Research Professor in the Artificial Brain Lab at Xiamen University, China; and general Chair of the Artificial General Intelligence Conference Series. His research work encompasses artificial general intelligence, natural language processing, cognitive science, data mining, machine learning, computational finance, bioinformatics, virtual worlds and gaming and other areas, Dr. Goertzel has published a dozen scientific books, 100+ technical papers, and numerous journalistic articles. Before entering the software industry he served as a university faculty in several departments of mathematics, computer science and cognitive science, in the US, Australia and New Zealand.
The field of AI started as a quest to build human level intelligence. But with time, the scientific community shifted more towards narrow AI. You have always been a strong supporter of AGI and strive to build a community around it. What according to you are the reasons why we deviated from the core belief of the field?
Until recently computing hardware was so weak that using it to implement advanced AI or even early-stage AGI, would have been very difficult. So researchers and developers gravitated toward things that were easier to do using the materials available. This is quite understandable, really... But what made less sense was the lack of understanding, on the part of many past AI researchers, that advanced AI and AGI WOULD be possible once hardware advanced a little further. This lack of understanding was resultant from the absence of a good, widely accepted theory of how intelligence works... Most researchers in the field STILL operate without any decent theory of how intelligent works, but now hardware is better, so many of them can get interesting results anyway!
‘Ten years to singularity’ is a book authored by you, released in 2014, in which you say that we are only 10 years away from the singularity if we really try. How likely is OpenCog to be the for-runner to meet that target?
OpenCog is a serious contender, but not the only contender. Positing exact numerical odds would seem silly. I would say our odds of being the “first to AGI” are above 20% and below 90%. If we manage to secure some solid funding this year, then that will shift the odds upwards. I think we have by far the best AGI design on the planet, but we are not the best funded AGI project on the planet -- not at this point anyway. So there are factors in our favor, and factors against us... if one wants to look at things as an “AGI race”, which isn’t necessarily the most useful perspective...
Is the architecture of OpenCog different from the way human cognition works? Or are they similar?
Similar but different! The high level architecture of OpenCog is modeled after human cognition, but all the particulars are different. The specific representational mechanisms are different, as are the specifics of the learning algorithms we use.
Can you explain the concept of cognitive synergy in brief?
To achieve a reasonable level of general intelligence using a practical amount of computational resources, a mind needs multiple cognitive algorithms associated with multiple types of memory -- and these algorithms need to be able to interfere subtly and appropriately with each others’ internals, so as to guide each other away from hopeless combinatorial explosions and other resource-wasting phenomena.
In your book, ‘The Hidden Pattern’, you’ve said that the mind is made of patterns. Can these patterns be recorded and transferred into a machine? Wouldn’t that be good way at emulating intelligence at the human level and beyond?
Uploading human minds into machines is technologically possible and almost sure to happen in the future. It will provide amazing new ways of living and thinking and feelings for human minds. On the other hand, uploaded humans are ultimately not going to be as intelligent as AGI minds that are NOT held back by the need to emulate the messy, constraining specifics of the human mind-brain...
Lets say, we get to singularity. What is something that may happen in such a situation that not too many people know?
The Singularity will be a Singularity of experience, just as much as one of technology. People will have access to states of consciousness and modes of awareness -- new ways of FEELING, RELATING, thinking and being that they cannot now conceive...
What do you think will be the reaction to an AGI with human level intelligence from a societal aspect?
People will love AGIs 1000x more than they now love their smartphones. Of course, there may be nasty or offensive or dangerous AGIs as well. But if AGIs are programmed and taught to be compassionate and helpful, people will bond with them emotionally and intellectually, very tightly... We bond very well with dogs and even toy animals, and they are not remotely as compassionate, responsive, helpful and interesting as AGIs will...
Also, say we apply AGI’s on the economic platforms; stocks, bonds, etc, replacing human brokers and investment bankers, how do you think the stock market will react to such an event and the effects of different AGI’s competing in the stock market?
Markets will become even more complex, and even less humanly comprehensible. And then the AGIs will modify financial markets into something quite different, something we may not even be able to recognize.... This may happen at the same time as the current money economy goes down the toilet, due to Drexlerian nanotech and other advances massively reducing the need to exchange work or money to support one’s everyday life...
The theme of our magazine this year is Kairos which refers to ‘ultimate moments’. As a futurologist, what do you think is the Kairos moment of the future bar a technical singularity?
An AGI that can hold a long conversation with you, giving a feeling of true understanding. A life extension pill that lets you live to 150 on average --- thus viably letting you “live long enough to live forever” (i.e. longevity escape velocity, the Methuselarity !! ...) A 3D printer that lets you print any ordinary matter you wish, by laying down arbitrary molecules in arbitrary specified relationships...
You recently visited India and even Mumbai. How did you like it here?
I love India! The food is great, and there is a great sense of life and humanity and passion. And so many people with interesting philosophical views. Definitely India is the philosophical capital of the world, which is pretty cool to me ... I love deep, inventive, creative thinking -- and India has lots of that...
Is there anything you’d like to tell the youth of the country?
The future is not just something that happens to us. It is ours to create. And national, racial and class boundaries are decreasingly relevant in the modern era. As one example, I helped found an AI/robotics company in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia (iCog Labs) and am consistently impressed with the smarts and skills of the best young technical university graduates there... Of course it’s more difficult to get in a position to make a huge science or tech contribution if you are raised in a poor village with low quality education. But it still is possible if you push hard enough and get a bit of luck. Society is still cruel and unfair in many ways, yet much less so than in past generations... The future will be created by everyone on the planet -- but most of all by those with imagination, creativity, strong will and the capability for hard work.
